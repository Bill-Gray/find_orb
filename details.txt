   Find_Orb uses the following details when none are provided
with the observations.  It only pays attention to lines beginning
with COD, OBS, MEA, or TEL.  In some cases,  you'll see a COM or ACK
or other line;  that just means I copied a header and didn't bother
to trim out parts irrelevant to Find_Orb.  (Though I suppose they
could prove useful at some point;  e.g.,  click on an observatory
code and get an address.  But please mangle e-mail addresses to
avoid spam problems!)

   Many details were automatically extracted from MPECs and merged
in.  See https://github.com/Bill-Gray/miscell/blob/master/details.c
for the code used to do that.  Such 'default details' are usually
not very trustworthy,  but the scope aperture may be useful in
computing exposure times.

   Details are occasionally date dependent;  one can specify that
a given observer/measurer was present between certain dates,  and/or
that certain telescope gear was used for certain date ranges.  See
details for (703), (E12), (G96) for examples.

   I've indented some codes/details by a space,  effectively commenting
them out.  That happens if I know those details are used at that site,
but that others also use it and I've no way of knowing which details
ought to be shown.  (For example,  (568) Mauna Kea has eleven scopes
and a bazillion observers;  I've no idea what to show there.)
